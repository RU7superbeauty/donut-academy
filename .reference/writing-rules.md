# Writing Rules — MANDATORY for all articles

## Style (emulate these two viral articles)
- **Roan (@RohOnChain)**: MIT quant course → prediction market mapping. Numbered phases, formulas inline, "here's why this matters for YOUR portfolio."
- **sysls (@systematicls)**: Agentic engineering methods. Opinionated, personal ("here's what actually works"), numbered rules.

Common traits:
- Strong opening hook (not "In this article...")
- Numbered phases with bold headers
- Formulas and code in context
- Practical mapping of every concept
- Long-form: 2000-4000 words
- Data-dense with specific numbers

## 🚨 CRITICAL: No Fabricated Data

1. **NO fake first-person claims** — Do NOT write "I've spent 18 months...", "in our testing...", "I've identified from backtesting...". Donut Research has not done these experiments.

2. **NO fabricated statistics** — Do NOT invent specific numbers like "87-92% accuracy" or "67% error reduction" unless you have a real source. If no source exists, use qualitative language: "significantly improves", "research suggests", etc.

3. **NO fabricated tables** — Do NOT create tables with made-up data pretending to be empirical results (e.g., fake GPT-4 calibration numbers).

4. **Real citations only** — When referencing papers, use ONLY papers listed in `.reference/ai-trade-frontier.md`. These are verified. Include the paper title, authors, and year.

5. **Clearly label estimates** — If a number is an estimate or projection, say so explicitly: "We estimate...", "Industry reports suggest...", "According to [source]..."

## Voice
Write as "Donut Research" — an analytical research team examining frontier topics. NOT as a personal blog. Use "we" sparingly and only for Donut's product decisions, not for claimed research activities.
